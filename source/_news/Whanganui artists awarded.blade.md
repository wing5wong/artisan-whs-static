<h3 class="element element-paragraph"><span>Abbey Hocquard won the Young Photographer Award &amp; Nathaniel Kirk won the Junior 2D Artist Award.</span></h3>
<p class="element element-paragraph"><span>Whanganui and&nbsp;</span>Ruapehu artists scooped plenty of prizes across age groups and categories at the Waimarino Art Awards this year.<br /><em>Photos / Supplied</em></p>
<p class="element element-paragraph">Whanganui artist Mike Marsh won the Life Study Award for his portrait of Castlecliff lawyer Jamie Waugh.</p>
<p class="element element-paragraph">"It is the first time I have entered the awards," says Marsh.</p>
<p class="element element-paragraph">"The portrait of Jamie is one of a set that included members of the Pussywhippers band.</p>
<p class="element element-paragraph">"I have done similar portraits of Jack Mitchell-Anyon, Matt Edmonds and Nick Ericcson."</p>
<p class="element element-paragraph">The Waugh portrait, has him wearing one of Marsh's bright, bold T-shirt designs.</p>
<p class="element element-paragraph">Three school-aged Whanganui artists also won category prizes in the awards.</p>
<p class="element element-paragraph">"I think its great that three out of the four Whanganui winners were under 18," said coordinator Paula Charlton.</p>
<p class="element element-paragraph">"The future is bright for Whanganui arts."</p>

<p class="element element-paragraph">Whanganui's youngest winner is 8-year-old Devika Mahdu who was awarded the Raetihi Primary School Junior Artist prize for her&nbsp;<em>Woodland Fairy</em>&nbsp;drawing.</p>
<p class="element element-paragraph">"We would like to acknowledge our youngest award winners," said Charlton.</p>
<p class="element element-paragraph">"Kobe Learmonth of National Park was awarded the Jenny Dekker prize for 5 to 9-year-olds in the 2D category for&nbsp;<em>Remember Them</em>&nbsp;and both these young artists really impressed the judges."</p>
<p class="element element-paragraph">The Waimarino Art Awards is open to all and the 2018 premier prize went to Taupo artist Sue Graham for her painting&nbsp;<em>He Ahi, He Tio</em>&nbsp;(Fire and Ice).</p>
<p class="element element-paragraph">Charlie Lynn of Taupo won the novice 3D award, Auckland painters Terry Prince and Jay Linden received awards, Raewyn Vanstone of Taumarunui won an open award and Steve Molloy of New Plymouth won the 3D award.</p>
<p class="element element-paragraph">Ruapehu winners are James Cannon who received the Best Entry from Ruapehu District award for&nbsp;<em>Tiki Auaha</em>, Marcela Diaz who received the new Remarkable Ruapehu award for&nbsp;<em>Ruapehu Sky</em>, Jahna Pekamu won a Junior Artist Award for&nbsp;<em>Elements</em>&nbsp;and Shannah Terpstra received a young artist award for&nbsp;<em>Destination: Nineveh.</em></p>
<p class="element element-paragraph">Photography prize winners were Phyl Cameron of Horopito who won the sport and leisure category for&nbsp;<em>Supporter Break</em>, Marama Groot of Ohakune won the junior prize for&nbsp;<em>Robin</em>&nbsp;and Suraya Hollands of Ohakune won both the novice and general awards for&nbsp;<em>The Darkest Nights</em>&nbsp;and&nbsp;<em>Haere Mai</em>.</p>
<p class="element element-paragraph">The final prize is the People's Choice Award which will be decided after visitors have cast their votes on Tuesday, July 17.</p>
<p class="element element-paragraph">The awards turned 15 this year and Charlton said the generosity of sponsors means that a total of $7500 was awarded to 2018 winners with a top prize of $2000 for the premier award.</p>
<p class="element element-paragraph"><strong>The Waimarino Art Awards exhibition is open for viewing at The Theatre Royal, 21 Seddon Street, Raetihi from 11am until 5pm and will close at 5pm on Saturday.</strong></p>
<p class="element element-paragraph"><em>By Liz Wylie</em><br /><em>Wanganui Chronicle 11/7/18</em></p>
